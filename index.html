Optimizing K-Means Clustering

Comparing TF-IDF and BERT for K-Means Clustering on GPT Reddit Comments: Dimensionality Reduction and Improved Centroid Initialization Strategies.

Details

    Explored two distinct methods for vectorizing text data: TF-IDF, a simpler method reliant on word frequencies, and BERT, a deep learning-based model known for capturing rich context and semantics. The aim was to compare their performance on our dataset.

    Conducted an alternative test by employing PCA and UMAP to reduce the dimensionality of BERT-derived features. This step becomes crucial as utilizing BERT often results in high-dimensional feature sets, making it challenging for K-means to effectively analyze all the features. By reducing dimensionality, we aimed to enhance K-means' ability to cluster the data effectively based on the reduced feature space.

    In another approach, selected initial centroids by leveraging the principal components. This method involves using the principal components obtained through PCA to initialize the centroids for the K-means clustering algorithm. By utilizing these principal components as starting points for centroids, we aimed to potentially enhance the convergence and performance of the clustering process, allowing for a more optimized arrangement of clusters in the feature space.

  • Tools: Umap, PCA, Seaborn, plotly
